The Story of Espresso


Espresso was born in Italy in the late 19th century, created out of a desire to make coffee faster and stronger. The word “espresso” means “pressed out,” referring to the method of forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under high pressure — producing a small, rich, and intense shot of flavor.

The Art of Perfection


Over time, espresso became the foundation for countless coffee drinks — from cappuccinos and lattes to macchiatos and mochas. Its signature golden crema and aromatic taste turned espresso into a symbol of craftsmanship, enjoyed by coffee lovers around the world.
